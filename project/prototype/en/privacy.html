Privacy Policy.

How we handle your personal data, who's responsible, and what your rights are.

Last updated: January 2026

1. Data controller

Confluencia Srl, Via Magenta 25, 20831 Seregno (MB), Italy. Email: info@dessertyfood.com.

2. Data we collect

We only collect data that's necessary to provide you with the services you request:

  • Contact details (name, surname, email, phone) when you fill in a form on the site.
  • Browsing data (IP address, pages visited, session duration) collected automatically via technical cookies.
  • Business details (company name, role, city) when you submit a partnership enquiry.

3. Purpose and legal basis

  • Handling contact requests — legal basis: performance of a contract or pre-contractual measures (Art. 6.1.b GDPR).
  • Sending commercial communications — legal basis: explicit consent (Art. 6.1.a GDPR), withdrawable at any time.
  • Legal obligations — legal basis: compliance with legal obligations (Art. 6.1.c GDPR).

4. Retention

Data collected through contact forms is retained for a maximum of 24 months from collection, unless different legal obligations apply. Browsing data is retained for 13 months.

5. Recipients

Your data is never sold or transferred to third parties. It may be shared with technical service providers (hosting, email marketing) acting as data processors under Art. 28 GDPR, bound by specific agreements.

6. International transfers

Any transfers of data outside the EU take place only with adequate safeguards in place (standard contractual clauses approved by the European Commission).

7. Your rights

You can exercise the following rights at any time by writing to info@dessertyfood.com:

  • Access to your data (Art. 15 GDPR)
  • Rectification of inaccurate data (Art. 16 GDPR)
  • Erasure ("right to be forgotten", Art. 17 GDPR)
  • Restriction of processing (Art. 18 GDPR)
  • Data portability (Art. 20 GDPR)
  • Objection to processing (Art. 21 GDPR)
  • Withdrawal of consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Italian Data Protection Authority (www.garanteprivacy.it).
