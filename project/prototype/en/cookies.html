What cookies are, which ones we use, and how you can manage them.

Last updated: January 2026

1. What are cookies

Cookies are small text files that websites save in your browser while you browse. They're used to remember your preferences, improve the browsing experience, and collect anonymous statistical data.

2. Technical cookies (necessary)

These cookies are essential for the site to work and don't require your consent. They don't collect information for marketing purposes.

3. Analytics cookies

We use anonymous analytics cookies to understand how visitors use the site (pages visited, time on site, referral source). Data is aggregated and cannot identify individual users. These require your consent.

4. Marketing cookies

We currently don't use profiling or third-party remarketing cookies. If we ever do, we'll update this policy and ask for your consent first.

5. How to manage cookies

You can manage or disable cookies at any time from your browser settings:

  • Chrome — Settings › Privacy and security › Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox — Options › Privacy & Security › Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari — Preferences › Privacy › Manage Website Data
  • Edge — Settings › Cookies and site permissions

Disabling technical cookies may affect how the site works.
